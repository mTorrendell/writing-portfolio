Our 24 languages are not a weakness. They're a shield.

Everyone treats Europe's languages as a problem. But they're one reason foreign software can't just walk in and take everything. One European company already proved that a language, done with care, can win against the giants. I want to write that story in the simplest words possible.

reading: Headline's AI Europe 100 (2026) · Europe 2031

The words deciding your future that nobody explained to you.

Sovereignty. Compute. Chokepoint. These words are shaping your life right now, and the people who understand them are only talking to each other. I want to keep building the glossary for everyone else. You can't want something you have no words for.

reading: Europe 2031 · my own glossary essay, where this started

When AI arrives at your work, who explains it to you, and in what words?

The first factories, farms and hospitals run with AI are already here. Everyone writes about the machines. I want to interview the people: what they were told, what they weren't, and how it felt. The same conversations I already have with artists and developers, taken to more places.

Farmer.Chat

Free AI assistant for smallholder farmers: send a voice note in your own language, no typing, no English required, and it answers. Over five million questions answered from 830,000+ farmers across Kenya, Nigeria, Ethiopia, India and Brazil. “This is the other story about AI. Not the hype: a voice note, answered.”

software

PROMPTS

In Kenya, a third of maternal deaths trace back to not knowing when or where to seek care. PROMPTS closes the gap with the humblest technology imaginable, a text message: 3.8 million mothers reached, 10,000 questions a day, an AI helpdesk that triggers fast hospital referrals.
