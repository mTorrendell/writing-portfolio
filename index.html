what I want to write next

Three pieces I want to make. Each one is a concept and a format at the same time.

The debate: one question, two chairs, plain words.

One question everyone is already arguing about. Two voices from very different corners answer it side by side, in short plain turns. I translate the jargon on the way in, and point out where they secretly agree on the way out.

The essay: our 24 languages are a shield.

Everyone treats Europe's 24 languages as a problem. I think they're a shield: one reason foreign software can't just walk in and take everything. Brussels quietly agrees, there's a European project building open AI models for all 24 of them right now. The idea came to me reading about why global companies struggle here. I want to tell it in the simplest words possible.

reading: OpenEuroLLM · Headline's AI Europe 100 · Europe 2031

The reflective interview: one conversation, one essay.

One long conversation with one person. Recorded, transcribed, then written into an essay that thinks around their best sentences: part profile, part reflection. The same muscle as my interviews, with my voice walking between the quotes.

PROMPTS

In Kenya, a third of maternal deaths trace back to not knowing when or where to seek care. PROMPTS closes the gap with the humblest technology imaginable, a text message: 3.8 million mothers reached, 10,000 questions a day, an AI helpdesk that triggers fast hospital referrals.

A DEBATE IN PLAIN WORDS

One question. Two voices from very different corners.

Short answers, plain words. That's a rule, not a suggestion.

Before each round, the jargon gets translated.

Between rounds, two lines on what was actually just said.

It ends not with a winner, but with the overlap.
